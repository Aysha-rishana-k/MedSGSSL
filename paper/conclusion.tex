\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented Anatomy-Constrained Masked Attention SSL, a framework that integrates rule-based lung segmentation priors into Vision Transformer pretraining for chest X-ray classification. By combining learnable anatomy-aware attention biasing, progressive masked reconstruction, layer-selective attention alignment, contrastive learning, and cross-dataset domain alignment within a student--teacher architecture, our approach steers the encoder toward diagnostically relevant regions without requiring any segmentation annotations. Experiments on the NIH ChestX-ray14 benchmark across 14 thoracic pathologies demonstrate consistent improvements over both direct classification and conventional SSL, with particularly pronounced gains in low-label settings. The ablation study confirms that each proposed component contributes meaningfully, and attention visualizations validate that the learned representations align with lung anatomy even after the anatomy bias is disabled during fine-tuning.

A limitation of this work is the reliance on rule-based segmentation, which can produce inaccurate masks for images with unusual contrast or severe pathology that distorts normal lung boundaries. Future work could explore learned adaptive segmentation priors, extend the framework to other imaging modalities with structured anatomy (e.g., abdominal CT), and investigate the interaction between anatomy-aware SSL and more advanced downstream architectures.
